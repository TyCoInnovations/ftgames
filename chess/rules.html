🎯 Goal
Checkmate your opponent's king.

♔ King
Moves 1 square in any direction.

♕ Queen
Moves any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

♖ Rook
Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

♗ Bishop
Moves any number of squares diagonally.

♘ Knight
Moves in an L shape: 2 squares one way and 1 square perpendicular.
Can jump over pieces.

♙ Pawn
Moves forward 1 square.
Can move forward 2 squares on its first move.
Captures diagonally forward 1 square.

⚠️ Special Rules

Check
Your king is under attack and must be protected.

Checkmate
Your king is under attack and cannot escape.
You lose.

Stalemate
You are not in check but have no legal moves.
The game is a draw.

Castling
The king and rook move at the same time.
Requirements:
• King has not moved.
• Rook has not moved.
• No pieces between them.
• King is not in check.
• King does not move through check.

En Passant
A pawn may capture an enemy pawn that just moved 2 squares as if it moved only 1 square.

Pawn Promotion
When a pawn reaches the last rank, it becomes a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

🤝 Draw Conditions
• Stalemate
• Threefold repetition
• 50-move rule
• Insufficient material
• Agreement between players

⏰ Chess Clocks
If your time reaches 0, you lose on time.

🏆 Tips
• Control the center.
• Develop pieces early.
• Castle your king.
• Protect your pieces.
• Look for checks, captures, and threats.
